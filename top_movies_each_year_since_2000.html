A Look into Top Movies Since the Year 2000

The film industry has witnessed remarkable success stories over the past two decades, with each year bringing forth blockbuster hits that captivated audiences worldwide. In this article, we'll take a trip down memory lane and explore the top-grossing movies from each year since 2000. We'll delve into the financial success of these films and assess their critical and audience reception.

2000: Mission: Impossible 2

Gross Amount: $546.4 million

IMDb RATING: 6.1/10⭐

Synopsis: Released in 2000, "Mission: Impossible 2", directed by John Woo, follows Ethan Hunt (Tom Cruise) as he embarks on a mission to stop a rogue agent from unleashing a deadly virus. The film was praised for its intense action sequences and Cruise's performance, although some critics found fault with its convoluted plot.

2001: Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone

Gross Amount: $1.02 billion

IMDb RATING: 7.6/10⭐

Synopsis: The magical journey of Harry Potter began in 2001 with the release of "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" (titled "Sorcerer's Stone" in the U.S.). Directed by Chris Columbus, the film introduces viewers to the world of Hogwarts as Harry discovers his wizarding abilities. It received widespread acclaim for its faithful adaptation of J.K. Rowling's novel and the performances of its young cast.

2002: The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers

Gross Amount: $948.9 Million

IMDb RATING: 8.8/10⭐

Synopsis: Peter Jackson's epic fantasy "The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers" continued the journey of Frodo Baggins and the Fellowship of the Ring. Released in 2002, the film earned praise for its groundbreaking visual effects, compelling storytelling, and seamless integration of characters. Critics and audiences alike celebrated its contribution to the fantasy genre.

2003: The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King

Gross Amount: $1.16 Billion

IMDb RATING: 9.0/10⭐

Synopsis: The epic conclusion to Peter Jackson's "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy, "The Return of the King", was released in 2003. The film follows Frodo Baggins and the Fellowship as they face the final battle against the dark forces of Mordor. It received widespread acclaim for its emotional depth, visual effects, and the culmination of the trilogy's narrative, winning 11 Academy Awards, including Best Picture.

2004: Shrek 2

Gross Amount: $928.76 Million

IMDb RATING: 7.2/10⭐

Synopsis: The animated comedy "Shrek 2" continued the adventures of the loveable ogre and his friends. Released in 2004, the film follows Shrek and Princess Fiona as they navigate the challenges of meeting her royal parents. With its humor, memorable characters, and clever pop culture references, "Shrek 2" became a massive hit and remains a favorite among audiences.

2005: Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire

Gross Amount: $897.05 Million

IMDb RATING: 7.7/10⭐

Synopsis: The fourth installment in the Harry Potter series, "The Goblet of Fire", directed by Mike Newell, was released in 2005. The film explores Harry's participation in the dangerous Triwizard Tournament and the resurgence of the dark wizard Lord Voldemort. It received positive reviews for its darker tone and mature themes.

2006: Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest

Gross Amount: $1.067 Billion

IMDb RATING: 7.4/10⭐

Synopsis: Captain Jack Sparrow (Johnny Depp) returned in "Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest", released in 2006. The film follows Jack's quest to settle a debt with the ghostly Davy Jones, leading to a series of high-seas adventures. While criticized for its complex plot, the film was a commercial success and contributed to the franchise's popularity.

2007: Pirates of the Caribbean: At World's End

Gross Amount: $961.70 Million

IMDb RATING: 7.1/10⭐

Synopsis: Concluding the initial Pirates trilogy, "Pirates of the Caribbean: At World's End" directed by Gore Verbinski follows Captain Jack Sparrow and his crew as they navigate through treacherous waters to rescue Captain Jack from the afterlife. The film received mixed reviews, with praise for its spectacle and criticism for its convoluted plot.

2008: The Dark Knight

Gross Amount: $1.03 Billion

IMDb RATING: 9.0/10⭐

Synopsis: Christopher Nolan's "The Dark Knight" (2008) brought a darker and more complex take on the superhero genre. The film explores the escalating conflict between Batman (Christian Bale) and the Joker (Heath Ledger), who delivers an iconic and posthumously Oscar-winning performance. "The Dark Knight" was praised for its storytelling, performances, and Heath Ledger's portrayal of the Joker.

2009: Avatar

Gross Amount: $2.924 Billion

IMDb RATING: 7.9/10⭐

Synopsis: James Cameron's "Avatar" (2009) set new benchmarks for visual effects and 3D filmmaking. The film is set on the lush alien world of Pandora, where a paraplegic marine, Jake Sully, becomes involved in a conflict between the indigenous Na'vi people and a mining corporation. "Avatar" received critical acclaim for its groundbreaking technology and became the highest-grossing film of all time until it was surpassed by "Avengers: Endgame" in 2019.

2010: Toy Story 3

Gross Amount: $1.067 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.3/10⭐

Synopsis: The third installment in Pixar's beloved franchise, "Toy Story 3" (2010) directed by Lee Unkrich, follows Woody, Buzz, and the gang as they face an uncertain future when Andy prepares to leave for college. The film received universal acclaim for its emotional depth, humor, and poignant themes of growing up.

2011: Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2

Gross Amount: $1.342 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.1/10⭐

Synopsis: The final chapter in the Harry Potter saga, "Deathly Hallows – Part 2" (2011), directed by David Yates, depicts the climactic battle between Harry and Voldemort. The film received praise for its satisfying conclusion, emotional resonance, and the performances of its cast.

2012: The Avengers

Gross Amount: $1.521 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.0/10⭐

Synopsis: Directed by Joss Whedon, "The Avengers" (2012) brought together Marvel's iconic superheroes, including Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, and Hulk, to save the world from an alien invasion. The film was lauded for its ensemble cast, action sequences, and successful integration of individual character storylines.

2013: Frozen

Gross Amount: $1.334 Billion

IMDb RATING: 7.4/10⭐

Synopsis: Disney's "Frozen" (2013) took audiences on a magical journey with the relationship between two royal sisters, Elsa and Anna, in the kingdom of Arendelle. The film's catchy songs, themes of empowerment, and stunning animation contributed to its immense success and cultural impact.

2014: Transformers: Age of Extinction

Gross Amount: $1.104 Billion

IMDb RATING: 5.6/10⭐

Synopsis: Michael Bay's "Transformers: Age of Extinction" (2014) introduced new human and robotic characters to the popular franchise. While the film achieved significant box office success, it received mixed reviews for its plot complexity and reliance on visual spectacle.

2015: "Star Wars: The Force Awakens"

Gross Amount: $2.071 Billion

IMDb RATING: 7.8/10⭐

Synopsis: J.J. Abrams' "Star Wars: The Force Awakens" (2015) marked the beginning of a new era in the Star Wars saga. Set 30 years after "Return of the Jedi," the film introduced new characters and continued the battle between the Resistance and the First Order. It received praise for recapturing the spirit of the original trilogy and introducing a new generation of characters.

2016: "Captain America: Civil War"

Gross Amount: $1.155 Billion

IMDb RATING: 7.8/10⭐

Synopsis: Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, "Captain America: Civil War" (2016) explored the internal conflicts within the Avengers as they divided into opposing factions over the issue of government oversight. The film was praised for its complex narrative, character development, and action sequences.

2017: Star Wars: The Last Jedi

Gross Amount: $1.334 Billion

IMDb RATING: 6.9/10⭐

Synopsis: Rian Johnson's "Star Wars: The Last Jedi" (2017) continued the story of the Resistance's struggle against the First Order. The film delved into the legacy of the Jedi and the complexities of the Force. While divisive among fans, it received positive reviews for its bold storytelling and visual spectacle.

2018: Avengers: Infinity War

Gross Amount: $2.052 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.4/10⭐

Synopsis: Directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, "Avengers: Infinity War" (2018) brought together the Marvel Cinematic Universe's heroes to face the cosmic threat of Thanos. The film was praised for its ambitious scope, emotional weight, and the culmination of storylines from previous Marvel films.

2019: Avengers: Endgame

Gross Amount: $2.80 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.4/10⭐

Synopsis: The climactic conclusion to the Infinity Saga, "Avengers: Endgame" (2019), directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, depicted the Avengers' final battle against Thanos. The film became the highest-grossing movie of all time, surpassing "Avatar," and received acclaim for its emotional resonance, fan service, and the way it concluded a decade-long narrative.

2020: The Eight Hundred

Gross Amount: $461.42 Million

IMDb RATING: 6.7/10⭐

Synopsis: Released in 2020, "The Eight Hundred", directed by Guan Hu, is a Chinese war epic set during the Battle of Shanghai in 1937. The film follows a group of Chinese soldiers defending a warehouse against overwhelming Japanese forces. "The Eight Hundred" became a massive success, especially in the Chinese market, and received praise for its historical significance and intense war scenes.

Spider-Man: No Way Home

Gross Amount: $1.921 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.2/10⭐

Synopsis:In the aftermath of Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019), in "Spider-Man: No Way Home" directed by Jon Watts, is where Peter Parker's identity as Spider-Man is exposed to the world by Quentin Beck aka Mysterio, Peter seeks help from Doctor Strange to make everyone forget his secret identity. However, the spell goes wrong, causing the multiverse to break open and dangerous villains from alternate realities to enter Peter's universe.

2022: Top Gun: Maverick

Gross Amount: $1.496 Billion

IMDb RATING: 8.3/10⭐

Synopsis: With over three decades of dedicated service as one of the Navy's premier aviators, Pete "Maverick" Mitchell finds himself exactly where he thrives—testing the limits as a fearless test pilot and evading promotions that would tether him to the ground. Tasked with instructing a group of new graduates for a unique mission.
