15 Reasons Why Growing Up in the 1970s Was Absolutely Awesome!

#1 Unforgettable Music

Image Source: Jusdafax, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons

The '70s were a musical odyssey, a symphony of iconic tunes that defined an era. From the disco beats of the Bee Gees to the soulful melodies of Marvin Gaye, every song was a journey through the vibrant soundscape of our youth. Oh and how could you forget the likes of Donna Summers, Fleetwood Mac, Stevie Wonder, ABBA (if that's your thing), The Carpenters, and the list goes on. The Rock n Roll, was on point. Who could forget Bohemian Rhapsody by Queen 1975, a super iconic hit. Aeorsmith, Led Zeppelin, The Eagles, so many musical talents to name.

#2 Groovy Fashion

Fashion in the '70s was a kaleidoscope of self-expression. Tie-dye shirts, bell-bottoms, and platform shoes were not just clothing; they were statements of individuality. The wardrobes were a vibrant canvas reflecting the spirit of freedom that defined the times. For younger folks, ever see some of your parents pictures of the 70s? Yeah they were rocking the bell bottoms, you know it.

#3 Cassette and 8-Track Tapes

Image Source: Rick Harris from Toronto, Ontario, Canada, CC BY-SA 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Before the digital age, music was a tangible experience. Crafting the perfect mixtape for a friend or cruising with the latest tunes on 8-track tapes brought a tactile joy to our musical journey, creating lasting memories with each click and flip.

#4 Iconic Toys

Image Source: Wikipedia

Our childhood was a playground of imagination, filled with iconic toys. From the magic of the Etch A Sketch to the epic adventures with Star Wars action figures, each toy sparked creativity and transported us to fantastical worlds. Some folks had the Evel Knievel 1st Issue Stunt Cycle Toy, since Evel Knievel really started coming to providence in the 1970s.

#5 Saturday Morning Cartoons

Image Source: Comicbook TV

Saturday mornings were a sacred ritual, a time to gather in front of the TV for animated adventures. Scooby-Doo, The Smurfs, Jabber Jaw, The Addams Family, and of course Josie and the Pussycats. These cartoons brought laughter and joy, creating a shared experience that united kids across the nation. What kid didn't look forward to Saturday morning cartoons with a lineup like that?

#6 Family TV Shows

Image Source: HuyNgan

Television was a communal experience, with shows like "Happy Days" and "The Brady Bunch" creating shared moments for families. These programs weren't just entertainment; they were cultural touchstones that brought generations together.

#7 Iconic Movies

The '70s delivered cinematic masterpieces that still resonate today. From the galactic saga of "Star Wars" to the suspense of "Jaws," going to the movies was a magical escape, a journey into worlds filled with wonder and excitement. The family would get together at family night and watch some great movies. I sometimes finding myself watching 70s movies. Not sure if anyone knows this movie, but "Marathon Man" was really good. Dustin Hoffman played in it and his brother was murdered in the movie and the people he was mixed up with thought he knew something. Now my taste of movies may not be everyone's cup of tea, and I could go on about 70s movies, so instead I'll link this Collider, 1970s movies ranked, which goes into some really good title of the era. Of course, when talking about 70s movies, who could forget Rocky. "Yo Adrean, we did it".

#8 High School Memories

High school in the '70s was a tapestry of self-discovery, a time of school dances, football games, and friendships that lasted a lifetime. The hallways echoed with the laughter of youth and the spirit of freedom that defined our teenage years. There's a Youtube who goes by the name "Jeremy" who really put a good video together on high school in the 1970s

#9 Muscle Cars

Image Source: 1974 DUSTER | by pauldagenais52

The streets were alive with the roar of V8 engines and the sleek designs of muscle cars. Cruising down the boulevard in a Chevrolet Camaro or Ford Mustang wasn't just transportation; it was a symbol of teenage freedom and rebellion. Today the only cool muscle car is the Dodge Challenger, but back in the 1970s there were a lot of them.

#10 Playing Video Games at the Arcade

Image Source: Wikipedia

The '70s witnessed the birth of arcade gaming, an era-defining experience. Games like Breakout and Space Invaders became cultural phenomena, captivating our hearts and igniting the competitive spirit that defined afternoons spent in dimly lit arcades. Kids would save up there quarters to play these fascinating games. This is also the time Atari really rose up in providence.

#11 Freedom of the Outdoors

Growing up in the '70s meant an era of unbridled outdoor freedom. Riding bikes until the streetlights flickered on, exploring wooded areas with friends, and engaging in spontaneous games of tag created a sense of adventure that shaped kids lives. Today everyone is on ther phone and sometimes I wonder if they know there is a whole big world out there to explore and have fun in.

#12 Roller Disco Fever

Image Source: Roller Skating Scene in Xanadu

Roller disco was the epitome of cool in the '70s. Gliding across the rink with funky tunes, disco lights, and the occasional disco ball added a touch of glamour to the weekends. Roller disco wasn't just an activity; it was a lifestyle, complete with neon outfits and dance moves that defined an era.

#13 Technological Pioneering

Image Source: Cornellanense (talk) 07:14, 29 October 2008 (UTC), CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Witnessing the birth of personal computing was an exciting part of growing up in the '70s. Early home computers like the Apple II and Commodore PET opened up new possibilities, laying the groundwork for the digital age we live in today. We really owe allot to computer scientist of that time, such as Bjarne Stroustrup who invented C++ and all the folks at Bell Labs.

#14 Drive-In Movie Theaters

Image Source: Jinx!, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Going to the drive-in was a magical experience. Piling into the family car, enjoying popcorn under the stars, and watching a double feature created unforgettable nights of cinematic bliss. The drive-in was more than a movie; it was a social event, a place where memories were made. They should bring back drive-in move theaters, this was an escape for teens. Of course, movies are just for the family, but also boyfriends and girlfriends. You bet a lot of romance happened during those days.

#15 Sense of Community

Perhaps the most enduring aspect of growing up in the '70s was the strong sense of community. Neighbors looked out for each other, and the local community felt like an extended family. Whether it was a block party, a school event, or just hanging out in the neighborhood, the '70s fostered a sense of belonging that remains cherished by those who experienced it. Back then you knew your neighbor.
